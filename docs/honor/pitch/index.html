The Pitch

Jun 24, 2018
4 min read

This is mostly inspired by the comments of the issue that I created in October 2015, where there are a few comments that mention creating a Kickstarter. As creating an entire fundraising effort around this seems to be outside of my comfort zone, I have instead setup what I like to call The Honor Jar.

The Elevator Pitch

This is how it works:

  1. You provide a donation to me, in exchange for a claim to one of my works of art .
  2. I work towards building Super.NET’s first application: a digital art exchange.
  3. If all goes well, your claim of art converts to a token which you can then sell on the digital art exchange.

I call it an honor fund as it is set up as a donation by design, which means no guarantees of success but my word that it will happen unless one of the identified risks occur.

My Story

I am currently dedicating the next two years of my life to building Super.NET and its first application: a digital art exchange. Although I do not think it will take that long, I am sticking to my strong feeling and internal philosophy that you simply cannot rush art.

My Schedule

I work every day for about 10 hours, or around 70 hours a week. I spend about three hours every day working out, one of those hours of which is on the treadmill at speeds ranging 3.5-7MPH, averaging at about 5 miles per day. I have been running for nearly a decade now, but this schedule has been in place for the past two years and I feel that I am “in the zone” in regard to this.

My Finances

With current investments and financial standing, I have zero consumer and business debt (and going for six years now) and my operating expenses are about $7,500 per year.

My Runway

At this current burn rate, I have about 3-4 years of runway at present, so, plenty of room and time. The problem being, however, is that it’s still burning my own capital and I would rather not do that if I can help it. I would rather accept donations for now and find a way of providing value back to those who donate in return.

The Honor Jar

So how does this work? It starts by providing a cryptocurrency donation to me.

What do you get in return for your donation? Well, if you like something from my art page, I will make you a sponsor for that work, meaning a claim goes to show that you have sponsored it, along with the amount that you donate.

You essentially sponsor the art that is there for now, and in turn provide capital so that I can undertake the efforts required here.

Now, what sits there currently on that site is currently my art, but I would like to extend this to build a digital art exchange that allows me (and other artists) to register and sell their art in a way that works much like what you see on Crypto Kitties along with a few other ideas that I have thought about developing into it.

This is what I will be building as the first “application” to Super.NET’s “framework.”

By providing a donation, you can claim a work of art from my art site. If and when I get this exchange built, this claim will convert to a token. Then – if and when the exchange is built – you will be able to sell it on this digital art exchange either immediately or at a later date which may or may not be worth something, such as art goes. My hope is that you are able to recover your donated value plus a return upon sale of this claim token on this digital art exchange.

I say if and speak in vague possibilities here because there are risks involved as with any venture. I am calling this the Honor Jar as it is based on a donation to me and I am giving my word to you that I will do everything I can to see to it that you see value returned back to you, but it is not a guarantee or promise.

Accordingly, there are risks that could interfere with my well-intended plans. In the spirit of being forthright and doing the right thing, I will now outline the risks that I have identified, followed by the potential rewards, and then provide the opportunity to donate if you still feel inclined.

Summary

Let’s check out the risks» or if you are already in it to win it, jump straight to donate now».

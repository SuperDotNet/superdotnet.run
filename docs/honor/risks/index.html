Risks

Jun 24, 2018
6 min read

The idea here is are providing a donation towards a service that I am building upon Super.NET that possibly could return value back to you in the form of a token that can be sold as value later on if this endeavor completes successfully. As with any business venture, there are risks to be aware of, even if this is simply a donation. I am keeping a list of possible scenarios that would interfere with that success here.

I May Die

Chances: Negligible (< 3%)

This is a super brutal yet incredibly real possibility. The whole reason I am even on this course is that I recently had what I call a brush with death that I discuss at more length here. Even so, it wasn’t even a “brush,” but more of a miss by a few minutes, which was enough to spook me and put me on the path where you are now currently able to read this.

Carpe diem, my friend. Carpe diem.

I May Burn Out

Chances: Negligible (< 3%)

I do not consider this a significant possibility, but again, it could happen. If anything, I take a little lull. A good example is my recent run-in with an extraordinarily complex performance issue that took a tremendous amount of energy to solve. I actually have not gone back to coding since that time as I have been busy with my art project and this site here.

For reference, I had another incident like this occur last year when I had to spend a good part of summer cleaning and organizing family belongings, which was what I would consider a “once in a lifetime event,” sort of like this adventure that I am on now.

I would say a good measure of my productivity and progress is demonstrated by my work with the ExtendedXmlSerializer. My first commit was November 26th, 2016 and it didn’t ship until November 17th, 2017. I mostly worked fulltime on that project with the schedule described above during this time minus a few client obligations with my business as well as a two-week vacation.

I May Quit

Chances: Negligible (< 3%)

I have never quit on any business venture in my life that had value to me. It simply is not my nature. I chalk it up to being taught to finish every plate of food in front of me. It’s good training for life. In the slight event that this does occur, I would like to think that I would find a way to pass this project off to a custodian along with remaining funds so that it will continue.

I May Lose Interest

Chances: Negligible (< 3%)

The only thing I can point you to is the open letter I wrote in 2011 that basically outlined exactly what this project can do now. That’s seven years ago. I can further point you to the vote that I established nearly three years ago which now sits as the most popular vote in all of the MSFT feedback ecosystem. From that, I am apt to say that I am not going anywhere, but who knows, maybe I will wake up one morning and inexplicably go:

¯\(ツ)/¯ “Meh”

Which reminds me…

I May Get Swept Away by a Black Swan Event

Chances: Negligible (< 3%)

I am putting this up there to account for the unthinkable. A corporation could sweep in and offer me a job doing something somewhere and I drop this altogether. This seems absurd to me because I could get a job instantly making money right now and I am doing this instead as I feel this is way more important.

Or, I could win the lottery and have more money than I ever know how to handle. That seems a bit silly because I would tend to think I would use that money to ensure the efforts here get completed and I honor those that support me.

Maybe I get swept up by aliens and never return? That is basically covered under “I may die,” so not that. Not sure what else, which is why it’s called a Black Swan and why I am simply leaving it as an identified possibility.

Another Project That Does the Exactly Same Thing but Better Emerges

Chances: More Real than Not (> 50%)

Microsoft might take a cue and finally put something together. Someone could get further inspired and one-up me in full, complete with a decked-out rock star team to show me just how wrong I am in my thinking and approach of preferring jewelers to these one-person-bands.

All of these are very real possibilities. What’s important to me is that the art exchange is created and that my art goes on sale there, along with other artists who can do the same. I also have some key ideas about this exchange as well that are required. I would have to examine the competing ideas and make decisions accordingly. I would further make these decisions on ensuring donors getting value back if I can help it.

But the way that I see it, I have been talking about this space for about three years now and – even with so much interest – no one has seemingly listened to what I have had to say about it (or rather, act upon it), so this might actually be a non-issue and not one that I am entirely worried about yet.

I May Get Overwhelmed

Chances: Possible (< 25%)

A part of me is cringing at the whole notion of going through all this trouble to even declare what I am working on, and considers it probably the most foolish thing I have ever done. Agent Smith would not approve, either.

The risk, of course, is that I get focused on other activities rather than coding which is where I feel I should be and find a great deal of joy in life – when the code actually finally works, that is. If others join up and want to help out, then these sorts of things become a real potential as a legitimate distraction to have to manage. However, I would prefer that I stick to keeping things simple and ensure that I build out the necessary, core pieces first and then bring others on to help flesh them out.

I May Not Meet the Schedule

Chances: Possible (< 25%)

This is the biggest risk, in my mind. It may take longer than two years to build this vision. It is incredibly ambitious and I have an especially particular vision for it so that means that I will be building it personally. However, if enough donors contribute to provide enough additional runway, then it’s back to being a matter of time and managing other risks. As long as you are OK with me working at my own pace with the schedule I outline above and eventually launch even if that takes longer than originally expected, then so am I.
